📚 Course Modules

📘 Module 1
CO: CO1 ⏱ 13 Hours
Introduction to Cooperative and Non-Cooperative Games

🎯 Major Learning Outcomes

  1. Apply fundamentals of game theory and rational choice.
  2. Analyze mathematical formulation of games and rational behavior.
  3. Examine non-cooperative vs cooperative games.
  4. Apply understanding of computational challenges.
  5. Analyze equilibria and learning techniques.
  6. Evaluate self-interest models in agents.
  7. Analyze applications of non-cooperative game theory.
  8. Apply mixed and pure strategies.
  9. Apply solution concepts to strategic interaction.
  10. Apply game theory to real-world rational decision-making.
  11. Evaluate models of choice under uncertainty.
  12. Apply fundamentals to strategic modeling.
  13. Apply reasoning under strategic interdependence.

📖 Theory Content

  • 1.1 Basics of Games, Strategy, Preferences, Payoffs
  • 1.2 Mathematical Basics, Rational Choice, Game Theory
  • 1.3 Basic Solution Concepts, Cooperative vs Non-Cooperative Games
  • 1.4 Basic Computational Issues
  • 1.5 Finding Equilibria and Learning in Games
  • 1.6 Self-interested Agents
  • 1.7 Application Areas in AI and Data Science
  • 1.8 Pure and Mixed Strategies
  • 1.9 Preferences and Strategic Interaction
  • 1.10 Rational Agents and Strategy Formulation
  • 1.11 Choice Theory and Model Applications
  • 1.12 Foundations for Modeling Decisions
  • 1.13 Interactive Decision-making Framework
📘 Module 2
CO: CO2 ⏱ 11 Hours
Classical Strategic Games

🎯 Major Learning Outcomes

  1. Apply strategic concepts to classical games.
  2. Analyze Nash equilibria in strategic games.
  3. Evaluate strategic firm behavior in economics.
  4. Analyze price competition using game theory.
  5. Apply equilibrium concepts in auction design.
  6. Analyze equilibria under uncertainty.
  7. Apply concepts to zero-sum games.
  8. Evaluate repeated strategic interactions.
  9. Analyze equilibria in extensive form games.
  10. Evaluate equilibrium refinement techniques.
  11. Understand computation aspects of game solutions.

📖 Theory Content

  • 2.1 Prisoner's Dilemma, Matching Pennies
  • 2.2 Nash Equilibria (Theory and Illustrations)
  • 2.3 Cournot's Model of Oligopoly
  • 2.4 Bertrand's Model of Oligopoly
  • 2.5 Auctions and Bidding Strategies
  • 2.6 Mixed Strategy Equilibrium
  • 2.7 Zero-Sum Games
  • 2.8 Repeated Games (e.g. Prisoner's Dilemma)
  • 2.9 Extensive Games with Perfect Information
  • 2.10 Subgame Perfect Nash Equilibrium
  • 2.11 Computational Issues in Solving Games

Games with Imperfect Information

🎯 Major Learning Outcomes

  1. Apply game theory to situations with hidden information.
  2. Analyze strategies in Bayesian games.
  3. Apply equilibrium concepts under uncertainty.

📘 Module 4
CO: CO4 ⏱ 10 Hours
Mechanism Design and Aggregating Preferences

🎯 Major Learning Outcomes

  1. Apply social choice theory to group decision-making.
  2. Analyze properties of aggregation mechanisms.
  3. Apply mechanism design principles.
